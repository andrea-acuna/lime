The Lime Story

2017

Company Founded

Lime (originally LimeBike) was founded by Toby Sun and Brad Bao in San Francisco with a mission to transform urban mobility with dock-free bike sharing.

2018

Electric Scooters Launch

Lime expands beyond bicycles to introduce electric scooters, rapidly deploying them across major US cities and beginning international expansion.

2019

Global Expansion

Lime reaches 100+ cities across 25 countries and introduces the Gen 3 electric scooter with improved safety features and longer battery life.

2020

Strategic Repositioning

Uber invests $170 million in Lime and transfers its Jump e-bike business to Lime, consolidating micromobility operations amid the pandemic.

2022

Carbon Negative Commitment

Lime achieves carbon negativity across its entire operations and unveils the Gen4 scooter with swappable batteries and improved durability.

2025

Today

Lime operates a diverse fleet of electric scooters, bikes, and mopeds in over 200 cities globally, with over 500 million rides completed on its platform.

About Lime

Lime is a global leader in micromobility, providing shared electric vehicles that offer sustainable transportation alternatives for urban communities. The company operates a multi-modal fleet of electric scooters, bikes, and mopeds that are accessible through a simple smartphone app. Lime's mission is to build a future where transportation is shared, affordable, and carbon-free, helping reduce dependence on personal cars for short trips within cities. The company employs a data-driven approach to deploying its vehicles strategically where they're needed most, while working closely with city governments to integrate micromobility responsibly into existing transportation networks. Lime has pioneered numerous industry innovations including swappable batteries, advanced vehicle diagnostics, and geofencing capabilities to enforce parking and speed regulations. As part of its commitment to sustainability, Lime operates a carbon-negative business model, powers its operations with renewable energy, and focuses on extending vehicle lifespan through robust maintenance and recycling programs.

Leadership & Vision

Wayne Ting

CEO of Lime

Wayne Ting joined Lime in 2018 as Global Head of Operations and Strategy before becoming CEO in May 2020. With a background at Bain & Company and experience as a Senior Policy Advisor in the Obama Administration, Ting brought a unique blend of business acumen and policy expertise to Lime during a critical growth period. Under his leadership, Lime has evolved from a venture-backed startup competing in a crowded micromobility market to a sustainable, profitable business focused on long-term city partnerships. Ting has championed Lime's environmental initiatives, including its commitment to carbon negativity and full vehicle electrification. He has steered the company through the COVID-19 pandemic by refocusing operations on core markets and emphasizing operational efficiency. Ting's vision centers on creating "15-minute cities" where residents can access most daily necessities within a short walk or micromobility ride, reducing car dependency and creating more human-centered urban environments.

Pioneering Sustainable Mobility

As of 2025, Lime's technology has evolved significantly to address the core challenges of micromobility: safety, durability, and sustainability. The current generation of Lime vehicles features advanced engineering designed specifically for the demands of shared use in urban environments. The Gen4 scooter incorporates dual handbrakes, enhanced suspension, a lower center of gravity, and larger wheels to improve stability and ride quality. All Lime vehicles now utilize swappable batteries, allowing operations teams to replace depleted batteries in the field rather than transporting entire vehicles to charging facilities—reducing the carbon footprint of operations by up to 70%. Lime's proprietary IoT technology enables real-time monitoring of vehicle health, allowing for predictive maintenance that extends vehicle lifespan to over 24 months, a dramatic improvement from early models. The Lime platform collects and analyzes millions of data points daily to optimize vehicle deployment, ensuring availability in high-demand areas while preventing oversaturation. Lime's geofencing capabilities allow cities to dynamically enforce no-ride zones, slow-speed areas, and designated parking locations. Through integration with public transit apps and services, Lime has positioned its vehicles as a critical first/last mile solution within broader mobility networks, helping reduce personal car usage for short urban trips.
